▸ Guide & key
Patterns Load a single pattern, or pick two sources and Blend them into a new groove. Sparse thins the result.
FX Warmth (asymmetric analog clipping), Ghost level, Delay (free ms or tempo-synced), and Room reverb — all after the drums, all off by default. Analog adds shift-register noise, inductor ringing, thermal drift and VCA bleed.
Pattern chaining A and B are two variations. Edit either, copy or swap them, and chain them A→B or A A B B.
Transport TAP sets the tempo by ear. Click is a straight quarter-note metronome (never swung, never exported). Clear All empties the grid and reactivates all 16 steps.
Sequencer Tap a step: off → on → accent → ghost. Accents hit harder — brighter, denser transient; ghosts are true soft strokes — darker and tighter, not just quieter. The blue master row jumps (disables) a step — that's how odd meters work. ⟳ Rotate flips the grid for phones in portrait: voices across, time flowing down — everything works the same, nudge wedges point down (late) or up (forward).
Mini-transport When the transport panel scrolls out of view, a slim bar appears at the bottom of the screen with Play, Stop, the tempo and the bank you're editing. Tap the BPM to jump back to the full transport.
Knobs Drag up/down to adjust. Double-tap (or double-click) any knob to reset it to its default.
Undo ↩ takes back the last grid edit — taps, gates, row tools, pattern loads, Clear All — ten steps deep. Ctrl/Cmd-Z works too. Tempo is never undone.
Row tools Hold (or right-click) a voice name: Solo silences everything else (playback and exports, like mute), ‹ › rotates the row — the classic move for clave and bell patterns, Copy then tap another name to paste the row there, Clear empties just that row, and Len sets the row's own length. A plain tap on the name still mutes.
Polymeter A row length under 16 makes that voice cycle on its own clock — a bell in 12 rides over a kick in 16 and they drift against each other, realigning every few bars. Dimmed cells beyond the length never play. Len 16 locks the row back to the bar. Carried into MIDI and WAV exports.
Fill ◆ arms bank B for exactly one bar — it plays at the next bar boundary, then the chain (or A) takes over again. Also on the mini-transport.
Pan Each voice has a Pan knob (double-tap recentres). Equal-power: a centred kit is bit-identical to before. The master WAV is now stereo with the image printed; stems stay mono.
MIDI out Pick a device and channel, then Notes sends every hit to hardware with the full feel — swing, nudge, dividers, humanize, velocity — using the same note numbers as the MIDI export. Clock adds 24 ppqn sync with Start/Stop. Settings stick per browser.
Save to file ⤓ writes the collection straight to a file you choose once — after that, one tap overwrites it. The plain ↓ download is still there. ↺ Reset gives you a fresh workspace (it asks once); your saved sound sets and songs survive.
Human Adds small random timing and velocity drift to every hit — the kick stays tight while hats breathe most, so the loop stops sounding quantised without falling apart. Re-rolled every pass, and baked into exports the same way % chance is.
÷ Divide Split one step into 2, 3 or 4 evenly-spaced hits. Ticks on the cell show how many.
«» Nudge Click once to arm late, again to flip to forward, again for off. Then tap steps for two intensities (~9ms / ~17ms at 120bpm). The wedge shows direction and strength.
% Chance Give a step a 100 / 66 / 33% probability of firing. Exports bake one roll, so the file is fixed.
Choke groups Tap a chip to cycle –/A/B. Voices in the same group cut each other off — but only when one lands after another, never when stacked on the same step.
Instruments Each voice has Level, Tune and Decay, plus a kit: 808, DR55 (Boss DR-55), CR78 (Roland CR-78) or FM (Syncussion-based, with feedback-FM snare and hats). Mix kits freely. Tap a name to mute it.
Save & recall Two kinds. A sound set stores only the sounds — every voice's level, tune, decay and kit, plus the choke groups and Analog — so you can drop a kit you like onto any pattern without disturbing what you've written. A song stores the whole workspace: both A/B patterns, the chain, all the sounds, the FX and the feel. ↓ Collection exports both to a .json file; ↑ Import brings them back. The app also auto-resumes — close the tab, come back, and your work is still there. Delete one with ✕ (it asks once to confirm). Presets persist in this browser; use ↓ Collection to save them to a .json file you can keep or move to another device, and ↑ Import to bring them back.
Export Pick a length — 1, 2, 4, 8 or 16 bars — and the chain repeats to fill it, re-rolling any % chance steps each bar so variations play out. MIDI carries timing, velocity, accents, dividers, nudges and odd-meter gating; WAV gives loop-aligned mono stems plus a stereo master. Both packages include an info.txt listing everything baked in — and, since warmth, delay and reverb are monitor-only and never printed, the exact FX settings to recreate the monitored sound.
